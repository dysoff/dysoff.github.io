  • Defining Self-Talk

So what is it?

Self-talk is that stream-of-conscious, ongoing
internal dialogue that runs inside our minds,
affecting our moods, behavior, self-confidence,
and appraisal of risk and reward.

It can be neutral, positive, or negative,
depending on the situation we find ourselves in

  • What Studies Say

Why does it matter?

Research has shown that self-talk:

  • Improves your self-awareness

  • Helps you to regulate and cope with difficult situations

  • Promotes you to be motivational to others.

If your inner dialogue tends to favor misery
and fuel anxiety, you may be experiencing
cognitive distortions.

    4 Tips to get started

Try these few
things to enhance
your self-talk

    Tip #1

Say what sounds right

Self Affirmations are a good start for improving
your self-talk, but if the statements don't sound
authentic to you, they'll do no good.

Find some affirmations that work for you, but add
your own self to them. Don't see it as a script.

    Tip #2

Talk to yourself using
“you” instead of “I”

By switching to your phrasing from “I” to “you,”
you can refrain from speaking to yourself in a
harsh way.

Additionally, think if you would say the
same thing to a person you care about.

“You got this, keep at it!”

    Tip #3

Pause and be mindful

Do you ever cringe or feel bad from an embarrassing memory from so long ago?

Take a deep breathe. Ask yourself,

“What is it I'm thinking of.”
“Can I let it go”

This will help identify whether you need to work
through a problem or if it's your mind
doing its job of processing information

    Tip #4

Don't stray from honesty

Make sure the words you say have some truth to them.

“I'm not the most skilled in
archery, but that doesn't mean I
shouldn't give it another shot.”

Admitting the things you are responsible for leaves room for
improvement and makes it easier
to accept positive self-talk
